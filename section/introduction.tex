\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The rapid technological advancement in railway systems is driving a fundamental shift from manual human control to automated and autonomous operations. This transformation is particularly evident in the pursuit of higher grades of automation i.e. GoA levels 3 and 4, where trains operate with minimal human intervention or become completely driverless. While highly automated urban transport systems like driverless metros have been successfully implemented worldwide, the extension of such automation to mainline and freight operations presents significantly greater challenges due to complex topology and increased risk of external influences.

As railway systems progress toward full automation, the role of remote control systems becomes increasingly critical. Remote control of trains is emerging as a vital layer of resilience in railway operations, providing essential fallback capabilities when autonomous systems encounter failures or degraded operating conditions. The ability to remotely operate and control automated trains, while maintaining communication with other technical and human stakeholders in the railway system, offers unprecedented flexibility in responding to disruptions and minimizing their duration. This capability is particularly crucial for intermediate levels of automation, where human operators remain essential for monitoring and intervention tasks.

The emergence of WebTransport as a next-generation web protocol presents a compelling opportunity to prepare modern remote railway control systems. Built on the foundation of HTTP/3 and QUIC (Quick UDP Internet Connections), WebTransport combines the reliability guarantees of TCP with the low-latency characteristics of UDP, offering bidirectional streaming capabilities that are particularly well-suited for real-time control applications. Unlike traditional approaches that rely on proprietary protocols or legacy communication systems, WebTransport provides a standardized, web-based solution that can leverage existing internet infrastructure while maintaining the performance characteristics required for safety-critical railway operations. 

The significance of developing robust remote control systems extends beyond technological advancement to address fundamental safety and operational concerns. The European Union has reported that significant rail accidents in 2020 resulted in an estimated total cost of approximately EUR 3.2 billion, highlighting the critical importance of resilient railway systems. Remote control capabilities serve as essential fallback mechanisms that can prevent or mitigate such incidents by enabling immediate intervention when automated systems fail or encounter unexpected situations. This is particularly relevant as the complexity of automated railway operations increases, where human operators may have advantages over automation in dealing with novel situations through their problem-solving skills and flexibility.

In this paper, we present the design and implementation of a comprehensive remote control system for real-time train operations based on WebTransport. Our approach leverages the protocol's inherent advantages to create a platform capable of handling concurrent control of multiple trains while maintaining the safety standards and operational resilience required for modern railway systems. The system architecture is designed to support both normal operations and emergency scenarios where remote intervention becomes necessary to ensure continued safe operation.

The primary contributions of this work include: (1) a working open-source and modern application of WebTransport technology to railway control systems, (2) a comprehensive system architecture that enables concurrent multi-train control, and (3) a performance evaluation of WebTransport's characteristics in the context of railway operations.

Our results demonstrate that WebTransport-based remote control systems can significantly enhance railway operational efficiency and safety by reducing communication latency, improving system responsiveness, and providing robust foundations for both normal operations and emergency fallback scenarios. The system successfully manages multiple concurrent train operations while maintaining the safety and reliability standards required for critical railway infrastructure, particularly in contexts where remote intervention serves as a crucial layer of operational resilience.
