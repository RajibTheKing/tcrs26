\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The evolution of railway transportation systems has consistently driven the need for more sophisticated control mechanisms to ensure safety, efficiency, and operational reliability. Modern railway networks span vast geographical areas, requiring remote monitoring and control capabilities that can handle real-time communication between central control centers and distributed train operations. Traditional communication protocols, while functional, often struggle to meet the stringent latency and reliability requirements of contemporary railway systems, particularly as train speeds increase and operational complexity grows.

The emergence of WebTransport as a next-generation web protocol presents a compelling opportunity to address these challenges. Built on the foundation of HTTP/3 and QUIC (Quick UDP Internet Connections), WebTransport combines the reliability guarantees of TCP with the low-latency characteristics of UDP, offering bidirectional streaming capabilities that are particularly well-suited for real-time control applications. Unlike traditional approaches that rely on proprietary protocols or legacy communication systems, WebTransport provides a standardized, web-based solution that can leverage existing internet infrastructure while maintaining the performance characteristics required for critical railway operations.

The integration of WebTransport into railway control systems represents a significant advancement in how we approach distributed transportation management. Current railway control systems often suffer from communication bottlenecks, protocol complexity, and limited scalability when managing multiple concurrent operations. These limitations become particularly pronounced in scenarios involving high-frequency control updates, real-time monitoring of multiple trains, and the need for immediate response to safety-critical events.

In this paper, we present the design and implementation of a comprehensive remote control system for real-time train operations based on WebTransport. Our approach leverages the protocol's inherent advantages to create a robust platform capable of handling concurrent control of multiple trains while maintaining data integrity and achieving the low-latency communication required for safe railway operations. The system architecture is designed to be scalable, allowing for easy integration with existing railway infrastructure while providing the flexibility to accommodate future technological advances.

The primary contributions of this work include: (1) a novel application of WebTransport technology to railway control systems, demonstrating its suitability for safety-critical real-time applications; (2) a comprehensive system architecture that enables concurrent multi-train control while maintaining operational safety and reliability; (3) an evaluation of WebTransport's performance characteristics in the context of railway operations, providing insights into its practical advantages over traditional communication protocols; and (4) a working implementation that serves as a foundation for future research and development in web-based transportation control systems.

Our results demonstrate that WebTransport-based remote control systems can significantly enhance railway operational efficiency by reducing communication latency, improving system responsiveness, and providing a more robust foundation for real-time control operations. The system successfully manages multiple concurrent train operations while maintaining the safety and reliability standards required for critical railway infrastructure.
