\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
    The rapid technological advancement in railway systems is driving a fundamental shift from manual human control to automated and autonomous operations. This transformation is particularly evident in the pursuit of higher grades of automation i.e. GoA levels 3 and 4, where trains operate with minimal human intervention or become completely driverless. While highly automated urban transport systems like driverless metros have been successfully implemented worldwide, the extension of such automation to mainline and freight operations presents significantly greater challenges due to complex topology and increased risk of external influences \cite{Shift2Rail2019}.

    As railway systems progress toward full automation, the role of remote control systems becomes increasingly critical. Remote control of trains is emerging as a vital layer of resilience in railway operations, providing essential fallback capabilities when autonomous systems encounter failures or degraded operating conditions. The ability to remotely operate and control automated trains, while maintaining communication with other technical and human stakeholders in the railway system, offers unprecedented flexibility in responding to disruptions and minimizing their duration. This capability is particularly crucial for intermediate levels of automation, where human operators remain essential for monitoring and intervention tasks \cite{CoganMilius2023}.

    The emergence of WebTransport as a next-generation web protocol presents a compelling opportunity to prepare modern remote railway control systems. Built on the foundation of HTTP/3 and QUIC (Quick UDP Internet Connections), WebTransport combines the reliability guarantees of TCP with the low-latency characteristics of UDP, offering bidirectional streaming capabilities that are particularly well-suited for real-time control applications. Unlike traditional approaches that rely on proprietary protocols or legacy communication systems, WebTransport provides a standardized, web-based solution that can leverage existing internet infrastructure while maintaining the performance characteristics required for safety-critical railway operations \cite{10.1145/3696355.3699698} \cite{mejías2025streamingremoterenderingservices}.

    The significance of developing robust remote control systems extends beyond technological advancement to address fundamental safety and operational concerns. According to the European Union Agency for Railways (ERA), the economic cost of significant railway accidents in the EU was estimated at approximately EUR 3.2 billion per year in 2021 \cite{ERA2023SafetyOverview}. This underscores the critical importance of developing resilient railway systems capable of minimizing such losses. Remote control capabilities serve as essential fallback mechanisms that can prevent or mitigate incidents by enabling immediate human intervention when automated systems fail or encounter unexpected situations. This is particularly relevant as the complexity of automated railway operations increases, where human operators may retain advantages over automation in managing novel scenarios through problem-solving skills and adaptability.

    In this paper, we present the design and implementation of a comprehensive remote control system for real-time train operations based on WebTransport. Our approach leverages the protocol's inherent advantages to create a platform capable of handling concurrent control of multiple trains while maintaining the safety standards and operational resilience required for modern railway systems. Our results demonstrate that WebTransport-based remote control systems can significantly enhance railway operational efficiency and safety by reducing communication latency, improving system responsiveness, and providing robust foundations for both normal operations and emergency fallback scenarios. The system successfully manages multiple concurrent train operations while maintaining the safety and reliability standards required for critical railway infrastructure, particularly in contexts where remote intervention serves as a crucial layer of operational resilience.

    \subsection{Contributions and outlines}
        This paper makes the following contributions:
        \begin{itemize}
            \item In this paper, we present a WebTransport–based remote control system designed for the real-time operation and monitoring of trains. The proposed system leverages modern web technologies to enable platform-independent access, allowing operators to control and supervise train functions directly through any standard web browser without the need for specialized software installations. This approach ensures greater accessibility, flexibility, making it suitable for both centralized and distributed control environments.
            \item We propose a comprehensive and scalable system architecture designed to support efficient multi-train control operations. The architecture is developed with a strong emphasis on flexibility and ease of integration, enabling seamless portability and compatibility with a wide range of sensors and hardware platforms.
            \item We designed the remote console interface which enables the operator to get a live view of the vehicle camera from the train and send control commands in real-time.
            \item We conduct a performance evaluation of WebTransport's characteristics in the context of railway operations, including video streaming and commands.
        \end{itemize}
