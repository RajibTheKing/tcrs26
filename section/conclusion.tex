\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
    In this paper, we presented a foundational design and implementation of a remote control system for train operations, leveraging the capabilities of WebTransport and the deterministic execution model of Lingua Franca. Our architecture integrates real-time video streaming and low-latency control command transmission over QUIC, demonstrating the feasibility of using modern web protocols to meet the latency requirements of remote train control. The modular design of our system allows for scalability and adaptability to various operational scenarios, while the use of Lingua Franca ensures predictable behavior and robust performance under real-time constraints. Our evaluation results indicate that the proposed system can achieve the necessary responsiveness for safe and effective remote control of trains. Future work will focus on further optimizing the communication protocols, enhancing the user interface for remote operators, and conducting more extensive field tests to validate the system's performance in real-world railway environments.
