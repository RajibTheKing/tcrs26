\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}
    Yu and Lee~\cite{YuL22} developed and evaluated a comprehensive remote driving system that integrates real-time video feedback with low-latency control commands over wireless networks. The system was implemented using the Robot Operating System (ROS) and communication was directly over TCP and UDP sockets. They demonstrated that latency—including video encoding, network transmission, decoding, operator reaction, and control command propagation—is the primary factor influencing remote driving performance and safety. Our work replaces ROS with Lingua Franca reactors to enable deterministic, time-triggered coordination, and uses a QUIC-based WebTransport protocol to support low-latency and bidirectional data exchange.

    Sun et al.~\cite{SunTsai25} explored the use of WebRTC for real time video streaming and MQTT for control command transmission in a teleoperated system. Rostand et al.~\cite{FezeuCZDVBS+26} also worked with video streaming over WebRTC. As they were working with autonomous vehicles, they did not focus on control command transmission. They compared the performance of video streaming over RTSP and WebRTC. Our approach leverages WebTransport, which provides a unified communication layer for both control commands and video streaming, eliminating the need for separate protocols and additional signaling infrastructure.
