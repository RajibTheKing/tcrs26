
Modern railway systems are moving toward autonomous operations, but remote control remains an important fallback for safety-critical scenarios.  However, the real-time transmission of
data and images is particularly crucial for remotely driven vehicles. The latency between a vehicle and the controller depends on the video streaming communication methods and transport protocols. This paper presents the design and implementation of a real-time remote-control platform for train operations based on WebTransport over HTTP/3 and QUIC. The platform leverages WebTransport's low-latency, bidirectional streaming to provide robust, concurrent control channels between control centers and trains while preserving message integrity and ordering. We describe the system architecture, implementation details, and the tools used to evaluate performance. In experiments with multiple concurrent control sessions, the open-source prototype demonstrates low end-to-end latency, reliable message delivery, and practical scalability—showing that WebTransport is a promising foundation for modern remote-control applications in railways.
